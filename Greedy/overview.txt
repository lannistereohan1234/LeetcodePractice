A greedy algorithm builds a solution piece by piece, always picking the option that looks best at the current moment, without reconsidering past choices or looking ahead.

Three key properties that define greedy:

Local choice — at each step, you make the choice that seems best right now.
No backtracking — once you pick, you don't undo it.
Hope for global optimum — you trust that local optima → global optimum.
⚠️ The catch: greedy doesn't always work. It only works when the problem has the greedy choice property
(a locally optimal choice leads to a globally optimal one) and optimal substructure.

Here are the 5 core patterns most greedy problems fall into:

#	Pattern	Core Idea	Classic Problem
1 --------- Sorting + Picking --------- Sort by some key, then pick greedily --------- Assign Cookies (455)
2 --------- Interval Scheduling --------- Sort by end/start time, pick non-overlapping --------- Non-overlapping Intervals (435)
3 --------- Jump / Reach Problems --------- Track furthest reachable point --------- Jump Game (55)
4 --------- Two-Pointer Greedy --------- Move pointers based on local optimal choice --------- Container With Most Water (11)
5 --------- Heap-Based Greedy --------- Always pick min/max from a priority queue --------- Task Scheduler (621)
